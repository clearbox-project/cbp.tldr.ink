Prospect Theory - Loss Aversion

Misbehaving Chapter 4 Value Theory

Described in Prosepct theory by Amos Tversky and Daniel Kahneman, a Nobel Memorial Prize in Economics winner (2002).

  • people tend to avoid losses more, than to seek equivalent gains.

Prospect theory discussed actual human behaviors in economic activities, with bounded rationality considered, versus the complete rationality assumed in Expected Utility Hypothesis (where people make decisions based on expected outcomes with perfect rationality).
