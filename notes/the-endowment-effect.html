The Endowment Effect

Misbehaving Chapter 2 The Endowment Effect

People are more likely to retain an object they own than acquire that same object when they do not own it.

Subjectively or subconsciously, we do not weigh opportunity cost the same as we do with out-of-pocket cost.

e.g. Purchasing gas at gas station with credit card will incur extra convenience fee. However, instead of "adding" this extra fee to gas price, it is marketed as regular full price, with small discount when you pay cash for gas.
